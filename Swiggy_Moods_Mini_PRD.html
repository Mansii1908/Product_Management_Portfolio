01

Executive Summary

We are building Swiggy Moods for young professionals living in Tier-1 and Tier-2 cities whose food ordering decisions are often driven by emotion and vibe rather than functional hunger alone. With rising disposable incomes and increasing screen fatigue among digitally native young professionals, there is a growing gap between what users feel in the moment and what Swiggy's current generic discovery experience offers them. Swiggy Moods bridges this gap by surfacing contextual, mood-based food recommendations that match the user's emotional state at the time of ordering. Success will be measured by a 35% session conversion rate within 90 days of launch, with an expected increase in average order value among our highest-spending user segment.

02

Problem Statement

When a working professional or a student opens Swiggy after a long day or during a get-together with their friends, the current experience shows them a generic list of restaurants and cuisines with no context for how they are feeling. This usually forces them to scroll endlessly without finding something that matches their emotional need.

How It Works

Food listings within each mood category will be populated through a combination of the user's past order data trends and a curated list maintained by Swiggy's restaurant partnerships team. After 3 uses, the feature will begin learning individual mood preferences and will automatically prioritise relevant restaurants and dishes for each mood.

06

Trade-offs

Risk
Price-sensitive users enter Swiggy Moods, see premium options, feel alienated, and leave the app without ordering.
Trigger
If the overall app bounce rate increases by more than 3% within the first 30 days post-launch.
Response
Introduce a price filter within Swiggy Moods so users can set a budget before seeing mood-based recommendations. This preserves the emotional discovery experience while making the feature accessible across all price segments.
